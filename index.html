LASERS CLEANING

Laser cleaning is a modern technology that uses intense pulsed laser beams to remove unwanted layers, contaminants, or corrosion from surfaces. It is commonly used for surface preparation before further processing, coating, or for the restoration of historical monuments. The technique involves precisely targeting a laser beam, which, through energy absorption, causes localized heating and subsequent sublimation or peeling of contaminants. Key parameters such as wavelength, pulse energy, and frequency ensure efficiency and minimize damage to the base material. The advantages include high precision, low abrasiveness, environmental sustainability, speed, and the possibility of process automation, allowing cleaning in hard-to-reach areas without the use of chemicals while minimizing secondary waste production. On the other hand, laser cleaning requires high initial investments and strict adherence to safety protocols, including the use of specialized protective goggles and operator training, as improper parameter settings can lead to material damage or pose risks to operators. In industry, it is used for cleaning machinery, removing corrosion from metal surfaces, and preparing surfaces before welding and painting. In the field of cultural heritage and artworks, it helps protect the original material layer without physical intervention. Technological development brings innovations such as more powerful and precise lasers, the integration of artificial intelligence for process optimization, and the expansion of applications to a wider range of materials, with an increasing focus on ecological aspects and sustainability. Laser cleaning represents an effective and gentle alternative to traditional methods such as mechanical grinding or chemical cleaning, and although it may be more costly, its advantages in precision, speed, and environmental friendliness make it an attractive choice for modern industrial and artistic applications.
